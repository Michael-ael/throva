Have questions? Find answers.

What is an options trading API?

An options trading API lets developers and traders access and execute options trades programmatically. With our API, you get real-time execution, access to full options chains, and support for multi-leg strategies. It’s perfect for building custom trading tools or automating your strategy.

Is the Public trading API free? Are there any fees to use it?

The Public trading API is free to use, and there are no fees for accessing or making calls. However, standard trading fees may still apply depending on the type of trade you place. For example, options trades include required regulatory fees. You can view a full breakdown in our fee schedule.

Can I trade options using the Public API?

Yes, you can trade both single-leg and multi-leg options with our commission-free API. Choose from market, limit, stop, and stop-limit orders to trade options in real time with flexibility that suits your strategy.

Can I trade equities using the Public API?

Yes, you can use the API to trade equities. Please review the documentation to learn more about setting up an API for equities trades.

What markets and instruments does the API support?

Our trading API is free to use and currently supports stocks, ETFs, and options trading, with index options, crypto, and bonds coming soon. You can also trade fractional shares and access extended hours trading for equities, up to 16 hours a day.

Does the options trading API support real-time data?

The Public trading API provides real-time quotes, account balances, and execution. You can monitor positions, track open orders, and review historical activity, all in real time.

How can I automate options trading using the Public API?

You can automate your options trading strategies by integrating the API into your trading system. Use it to place real-time trades, set conditions for entry/exit, and analyze option chains by symbol, expiration, type, and more, all with no commissions.

Can I trade Crypto using the Public API?

Crypto trading via the API is coming soon. Join our discord server to receive the latest updates.

Can I use the API with an international account?

There are certain restrictions placed on international accounts. Non US resident or US entity? Please reach out to our support team to verify if you are eligible: Concierge@public.com

Can I trade with my corporate account?

Yes, but certain restrictions may apply. Please reach out to our support team to verify if you are eligible: Concierge@public.com

Is the Public trading API suitable for developers and quants?

Yes! The API is designed for developers, algorithmic traders, and quantitative analysts who want low-latency access and high customization. Whether you’re backtesting or deploying live trades, Public offers a robust platform to build on.

Bond Accounts
A Bond Account is a self-directed brokerage account with Public Investing. Deposits into this account are used to purchase 10 investment-grade and high-yield bonds. The Bond Account’s yield is the average, annualized yield to worst (YTW) across all ten bonds in the Bond Account, before fees. A bond’s yield is a function of its market price, which can fluctuate; therefore a bond’s YTW is not “locked in” until the bond is purchased, and your yield at time of purchase may be different from the yield shown here. The “locked in” YTW is not guaranteed; you may receive less than the YTW of the bonds in the Bond Account if you sell any of the bonds before maturity or if the issuer defaults on the bond. Public Investing charges a markup on each bond trade. See our Fee Schedule.

Bond Accounts are not recommendations of individual bonds or default allocations. The bonds in the Bond Account have not been selected based on your needs or risk profile. JIKO SECURITIES, INC.

Jiko Accounts
Jiko Securities, Inc. (“JSI”), a registered broker-dealer and member of FINRA & SIPC, provides accounts (“Jiko Accounts”) offering 6-month US Treasury Bills (“T-bills”). See JSI’s FINRA BrokerCheck and Form CRS for further information. For the avoidance of doubt, a Jiko Account is different and separate from the Treasury Account offered by Public Investing and advised by Public Advisors (see “Treasury Accounts” section above).

JSI uses funds from your Jiko Account to purchase T-bills in increments of $100 “par value” (the T-bill’s value at maturity). T-bills are purchased at a discount to the par value and the T-bill’s yield represents the difference in price between the “par value” and the “discount price.” Aggregate funds in your Jiko Account in excess of the T-bill purchases will remain in your Jiko Account as cash. The value of T-bills fluctuate and investors may receive more or less than their original investments if sold prior to maturity. T-bills are subject to price change and availability - yield is subject to change. Past performance is not indicative of future performance. Investments in T-bills involve a variety of risks, including credit risk, interest rate risk, and liquidity risk. As a general rule, the price of a T-bills moves inversely to changes in interest rates. Although T-bills are considered safer than many other financial instruments, you could lose all or a part of your investment. See Jiko U.S. Treasuries Risk Disclosures for further details.
